# Phase 1: Niche Research & Competitive Intelligence

## Purpose

Before creating a single frame of content, identify the highest-value niche where the user's interests intersect with market opportunity. This phase produces a data-driven niche scorecard, competitive landscape map, and defensible positioning statement.

---

## Niche Scoring Framework

Evaluate every candidate niche across these 6 dimensions. Use web search to pull current data for each.

### Scoring Matrix (1-10 scale each, weighted)

| Dimension | Weight | What to Measure |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **CPM Potential** | 25% | Average CPM in the niche. Finance/tech/B2B = $15-45. Entertainment/gaming = $2-6. Health/education = $8-18. Search: "[niche] youtube CPM 2025/2026" |
| **Audience Size** | 20% | Total addressable audience. Check search volume for core keywords, subreddit sizes, hashtag volume. Bigger isn't always better â a 500K passionate audience > 50M casual viewers |
| **Production Ease** | 20% | Can this be produced with AI tools alone? Stock footage availability. Visual complexity. Voiceover requirements. Rate: 10 = pure AI generation, 1 = requires live filming |
| **Competition Density** | 15% | Number of channels over 100K subs in this exact sub-niche. Saturation level. Rate: 10 = wide open, 1 = dominated by established players |
| **Monetization Paths** | 10% | Beyond AdSense: affiliate potential, sponsorship demand, digital product fit, course potential. More paths = higher score |
| **Trend Trajectory** | 10% | Is this growing, stable, or declining? Use Google Trends data. Emerging niches score highest |

### Niche Evaluation Process

1. **Generate 15-20 candidate niches** from user's interests crossed with high-CPM categories
2. **Score each** across the 6 dimensions using web research
3. **Rank by weighted score** and present the top 10
4. **Deep-dive the top 3** with full competitive analysis

### Output Format: Niche Scorecard

```markdown

**Weighted Score: X.X / 10**

| Dimension | Score | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| CPM Potential | X/10 | Average CPM: $XX. Source: [data point] |
| Audience Size | X/10 | Core keyword volume: XXK/mo. Subreddit: XXK members |

```

## Sub-Niche Identification

The biggest channels don't compete in broad niches. They own sub-niches. Use this framework:

### The Niche Narrowing Ladder

```
Level 1 (Too Broad): "Finance"
Level 2 (Still Broad): "Personal Finance"
Level 3 (Getting Specific): "Personal Finance for Millennials"
Level 4 (Sub-Niche): "FIRE Movement for Tech Workers"
Level 5 (Micro-Niche): "Coast FIRE with Index Funds for Remote Workers"
```

**Target Level 3-4** for new channels. Level 5 is too narrow for growth; Level 1-2 is too competitive.

### Sub-Niche Validation Checklist

- [ ] Can you generate 50+ unique video titles without repeating core concepts?
- [ ] Are there at least 3 keywords with 10K+ monthly searches?
- [ ] Is there at least one competitor under 50K subs growing fast?
- [ ] Can the content be produced with AI tools (no live footage required)?
- [ ] Does the audience have purchasing power (for monetization)?
- [ ] Is there an adjacent community (subreddit, Discord, Facebook group) with 10K+ active members?

## Positioning Statement

After niche selection, produce a positioning statement that guides ALL future content decisions.

### Formula

```
For [target audience] who [specific need/frustration],
[Channel Name] is the [content format] channel that [unique value proposition]
unlike [competitor approach] because [defensible differentiator].
```

### Example

```
For mid-career professionals who want to understand AI's impact on their industry,
TechPulse Daily is the 8-minute animated explainer channel that translates complex AI research into career-actionable insights,
unlike generic tech news channels because every video ends with a specific "what to do Monday morning" action item.
```

## Cross-Platform Niche Adaptation

The same niche manifests differently across platforms. Document how the niche translates:

| Platform | Content Angle | Format | Audience Segment |
|----------|--------------|--------|-----------------|
| YouTube (long) | Deep dives, tutorials, analysis | 8-15 min | Learners, researchers |
| YouTube Shorts | Quick tips, surprising facts, hooks | 30-60 sec | Discovery audience |
| Instagram Reels | Visual storytelling, lifestyle angle | 15-60 sec | Aspirational viewers |
| TikTok | Trend-riding, contrarian takes, duets | 15-90 sec | Younger, trend-aware |
| X/Twitter | Commentary, threads, hot takes | 30-120 sec video + text | Professionals, debate-seekers |
| Facebook | Shareable, emotional, community | 3-5 min | 35+ demographic, sharers |
| LinkedIn | Professional insight, career relevance | 1-3 min | B2B, career-focused |
